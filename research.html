1. On graded rings with homogeneous derivations

Revista de la Unión Matemática Argentina

DOI
› abstract
The concept of homogeneous derivations was introduced by Kanunnikov (2018). In this paper, we establish interesting results related to homogeneous derivations. First, we prove the existence of a non-trivial family of derivations that are not homogeneous on graded rings. Furthermore, based on homogeneous derivations, we extend certain existing significant results in the context of prime (resp. semiprime) rings to gr-prime (resp. gr-semiprime) rings, such as Posner's and Herstein's theorems.
2. Generalized homogeneous derivations of graded rings
[arxiv]
› abstract
In this manuscript, we establish a comprehensive theory of generalized homogeneous derivations on graded rings, presenting a unifying framework that extends Kanunnikov's homogeneous derivations. For nontrivially graded rings, we demonstrate through explicit counterexamples that homogeneous derivations constitute a proper subclass of generalized homogeneous derivations. Our investigation employs categorical methods to elucidate the underlying algebraic structures. This approach yields functorial relationships between graded rings and their derivation sets, producing isomorphism invariants and structural decomposition theorems. This functorial perspective allows for a systematic examination of their behavior under various algebraic operations, including direct products, polynomial extensions, and tensor products. As applications, we establish conditions for the commutativity of gr-prime rings using generalized homogeneous derivations, characterize when gr-semiprime rings contain non-trivial central graded ideals, and extend key results from prime and semiprime ring theory to graded settings. Furthermore, we extend the theory to graded modules and define functorial categories capturing their derivation structures.
3. Differential Structures in Graded Rings and Modules
› abstract
In this paper, we establish a comprehensive theory of degree-preserving derivations (dp-derivations) that naturally extend classical derivations to graded setting. We elucidate the essential structural properties of dp-derivations, with particular emphasis on their action on graded ideals and the integrality characteristics of their inverse images. For commutative graded rings equipped with a dp-derivation, we demonstrate that the inverse image of any gr-prime differential ideal is integrally closed. Our investigation of differential simplicity yields that for $\delta$-gr-simple rings of prime characteristic, the gr-nilradical necessarily constitutes the unique gr-prime ideal. We further establish that the dp-skew polynomial ring over a gr-Noetherian ring preserves the gr-Noetherian property, thus extending the Hilbert basis theorem to differential graded contexts. Finally, we generalize our framework to graded modules, developing a comprehensive categorical theory of dp-modules through an extension of dp-derivations to the context of graded modules.
