About

I am a PhD student in Mathematics at the University of Sherbrooke, where I work under the supervision of Dr. Maxence Mayrand. My research focuses on several interconnected areas of modern mathematics.

Prior to my current position, I was a doctoral student at the University of Sidi Mohamed Ben Abdellah, where I worked under the guidance of Prof. Lahcen Oukhitte.

Research Interests

My research interests span several areas of mathematics, with particular emphasis on:

Lie Theory Poisson Geometry Symplectic Geometry Ring Theory

Research Projects

Lie Algebras and Poisson Structures

Investigating the relationship between Lie algebras and Poisson structures on manifolds, with applications to integrable systems and mathematical physics.

Symplectic Geometry Applications

Exploring applications of symplectic geometry in classical mechanics and quantum field theory.

Ring Theory and Algebraic Structures

Research on special classes of rings and their applications in algebraic geometry and representation theory.

Current Work

My current research focuses on the intersection of Lie theory and Poisson geometry, particularly examining how certain algebraic structures can be understood through geometric methods.
