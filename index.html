Introduction

I am a PhD student in Mathematics at the Université de Sherbrooke, where I work under the supervision of Maxence Mayrand. My research focuses on advancing our understanding of fundamental mathematical structures and their applications.

Before joining Université de Sherbrooke, I was a doctoral student at the University of Sidi Mohamed Ben Abdellah, where I worked under the supervision of Lahcen Oukhitte. This foundational work provided me with a strong background in advanced mathematical theory and research methodology.

Research Interests

Algebraic Geometry Number Theory Mathematical Physics Topology Analysis Differential Equations

Current Work

My current research explores the intersection of modern algebraic methods with classical analysis, seeking to develop new frameworks that can address long-standing problems in mathematical physics and geometry. I am particularly interested in how abstract mathematical concepts can provide insights into physical phenomena.
